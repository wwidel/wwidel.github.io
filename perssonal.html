2. Nonhuman animals other than Hugo

Domestic chickens are highly inteligent animals that exhibit complex social behaviours (e.g., deception, learning from others) as well as some evidence for empathy [1, 2].
The same goes for cows and pigs. Plus, they suffer from loss of their children, and the children development is severely perturbed by the separation from their mother [3, 4].
Whether reared for their flesh or their milk, most of the livestock live lifes not worth living, filled with discomfort and suffering [4, 5, 6].

If you consider this to be an issue, then you can try to reduce the presence of animal products in your diet, perhaps even remove them completely, or at least select producers that ensure a high degree of animal welfare on their farms.

3. Other

If you expect that you or your offspring will be alive in 2050, you might be worried about the growing negative impact of the humankind on the planet.

If you are not, then you can have a look at the World Scientists' Warnings to Humanity: 1992, 2017 (only a couple of minutes of reading!), or at the recent (May 2019) IPBES Global Assessment Report on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services: report's overview, detailed summary.

If you feel like doing something about this, you might try putting pressure on policy makers. Perhaps  Extinction rebellion is an option.
